    History of Beekeeping

    Egyption records show that bees were provided with domiciles and kept for honey as early as 3000BC. The products that bees produced were so saught after that the Romans implemented taxes on honey and beeswax. Honeybees were traditionally(and still are in certain places) kept in clay pots, hollow logs, wicker baskets, and straw hives. However, the amount of honey produced by these methods were subpar, this lead to the development of the modern day moveable-frame hive. It is important to note that beekeeping is not restricted to keeying honeybees. In Central and South America, traditional beekeeping for honey has been practiced for thousands of years with "stingless" bees (Meliponini). Beekeeping also includes the keeping of bees for the purpose of crop pollination.

    Bees are extremely fuzzy and generally friendly if you don't get to close to their hive unlike their close cousins the wasp. Wasps sting you for fun but not bees! Bees will only sting you if you've done something to anger them so if you've ever been stung by a bee I guess that means you're not a very nice person :^)
